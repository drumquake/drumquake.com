Sander Owen is a Norwegian drummer and music producer, living in the USA.

He has played with Kevin Moore (Dream Theater), Chris Poland (Megadeth), Matt Malley (Counting Crows), Ric Fierabracci (Chick Corea), Brett Garsed (Nelson), Ben Mallare (Kelly Clarkson), Vartan Mkhitaryan (Apple Pie), Marie Bergman (the Swedish artist), and The Gravity Guild (top ten Hollywood band on ReverbNation); and he has warmed up for Machina (ex Evanescence members).

He has also produced music for The Red Cross, Keith Floyd (the famous TV chef), TV Norge, and Norsk Film. In Los Angeles, he has performed at The Whisky a Go Go, The Baked Potato, Musician's Institute, and more.

He started playing the drums at age five, because no one else wanted to be the drummer in his older brothers Kiss cover band. One of their neighbors took Sander under his wings, and showed him the basics.

He quickly fell in love with the instrument, and started learning from the best—Omar Hakim, Manu Katche, Jeff Porcaro, Dennis Chambers, Peter Erskine, Stewart Copeland, Dave Weckl, Steve Gadd, Bernard Peardy, Jimmy Cobb, Billy Higgins, Billy Cobham.

At fifteen, he started doing professional work—cruise ships, wedding bands, cover bands. He went on to study music at the SVG School under the Norwegian jazz drummer Audun Kleive (Terje Rypdal). He also had many students of his own. He composed and arranged music for theaters and revues as well.

After graduation, he moved to Oslo and started working for Knut Halmrast Sound Production. He also had a small, but successful rehearsal studio business at the time. He started a band; They released two albums and five singles.

Eventually, he moved to Los Angeles.
