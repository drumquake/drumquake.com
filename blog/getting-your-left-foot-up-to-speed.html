GETTING YOUR LEFT FOOT UP TO SPEED

2016/05/23

When you first start out with double bass drums, you’re likely to be discouraged by your weak non-dominant foot. Even worse, it's hard to get it up to par.

This is something I can relate to. I didn’t start using a double until a couple of years ago. It's not up to par with my dominant foot, but it's beginning to yield the results I was aiming for:

EXERCISE

Here's some exercises to get you up to speed 1. Practice them like you would 360 Paradiddles:

  • LLLL
  • RLRL
  • RRLL
  • rlRL

    • They may seem simple, but it doesn't really take that much left foot independence to play the most common stuff.

    You’ve probably never thought about how much exercise your right foot has gotten over the years—your left foot is just starting out—so give it a few weeks before you expect results.

    1.R=right foot; L=left foot; r=right arm; l=left arm.
