DRUM TECHNIQUE: WHERE TO BEGIN?

2016/04/15

The sheer scope of practice material out there is paralyzing. You want to consume it all, but you just don’t have the time.

Much of it, however, is just variations over the same principles—360 paradiddles1 being one of the most essential.

360 PARADIDDLES

As you probably know, there are 4 paradiddles (L = left hand, R = right hand):

  • RLRR LRLL
  • RLLR LRRL
  • RRLR LLRL
  • RLRL LRLR

These can be played with endless variations—varying between feet and arms; with and without rests; with and without accents; hitting different drums; playing them backwards; mixing together half of two different paradiddles.

EXERCISE ROUTINE

Pick an exercise; start playing it slow; gradually increase the tempo until just before you start stumbling; reverse it; go back down; repeat three times:

Make up a new exercise every time! The goal is not to get good at one variation at the time, but to improve your overall coordination. Here are some examples of variations you may try (l = leg, L = arm, ! = break; () = accent):

  • RLRR lrll
  • RllR LrrL
  • lRRl RL!R
  • r(r)Lr l(l)Rl

CONCLUSION

If you do this simple exercise two or three times per week, you’re likely to be way ahead of most drummers a couple of years from now.

1. It’s been around for decades; I just put a name to it.
