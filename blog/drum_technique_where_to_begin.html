DRUM TECHNIQUE: WHERE TO BEGIN?

2016/04/15

The sheer scope of practice material out there is paralyzing: You want to consume it all, but you just don’t have the time.

Much of it, however, is just variations over the same principles. 360 paradiddles1 is one of the most essential of these principles.

360 PARADIDDLES

As you probably know, there are 4 paradiddles (L = left hand, R = right hand):

  • RLRR LRLL
  • RLLR LRRL
  • RRLR LLRL
  • RLRL LRLR

These can be played with endless variations—with or without rests and accents; varying between arms and feet; hitting different drums; playing them backwards; mixing together half of two paradiddles.

Integrating 360’s into your playing makes a big difference:

Drummers who have never practiced 360’s, often sound more like this:

EXERCISE ROUTINE

Pick an exercise; start playing it slowly; gradually increase the tempo until just before you start stumbling; reverse it; go back down; repeat three times:

Make up a new exercise every time! The goal is not to get good at one variation at the time, but to improve your overall coordination.

Here are some examples of variations you may try (l = leg, L = arm, ! = break; () = accent):

  • RLRR lrll
  • RllR LrrL
  • lRRl RL!R
  • r(r)Lr l(l)Rl

CONCLUSION

If you do a 360 two or three times per week, you’re likely to be way ahead of most drummers a couple of years from now.
